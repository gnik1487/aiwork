## shortContent
Few books changed mainstream thinking as deeply as *Thinking, Fast and Slow*. Daniel Kahneman organized decades of behavioral research into a practical model: two cognitive systems that shape judgment.

System 1 is fast, intuitive, and automatic. It helps us make quick calls in daily life. System 2 is deliberate, analytical, and effortful. It activates when problems become complex or unfamiliar. Both systems are useful, but they can conflict—and that conflict explains many predictable mistakes.

The book’s power comes from application. Leaders use it to improve hiring and strategy decisions. Product teams use it to design clearer user journeys. Individuals use it to spot cognitive biases in money, career, and relationships.

The key lesson is not to distrust intuition entirely. Instead, it’s to know when intuition is enough and when deeper reasoning is needed. Better decisions often come from designing processes that reduce bias before choices are made.
